// Why Sigmoid?

Sigmoid maps any number to (0,1) — perfect for probabilities. Its derivative is beautiful: σ'(z) = σ(z)·(1−σ(z)). This means we only need the output value to compute the gradient — super efficient for backprop.

// Why Squared Loss?

L = (y−a)² penalizes big mistakes much more than small ones (quadratic!). Its gradient ∂L/∂a = −2(y−a) is simple and points directly at the error. Larger error → larger gradient → bigger step to fix it.

// Why Learning Rate?

Too large η → overshoot, weights explode. Too small → learning crawls. The sweet spot lets the network descend the loss landscape smoothly. Try η=0.9 vs η=0.01 in Auto Train and watch the difference!
